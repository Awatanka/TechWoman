The best Places to take a Photo

Photography can serve many purposes and have many facets. Photography can tell a story, it can capture a moment in time, it can document, and it can be art. Getting out of the house, walking, and even hiking are common side effects of interest in photography. Do you like landscape photography? Then you’ll need to go where the landscapes are, and that often means some form of exercise. Do you want to photograph people? Then you’ll need to walk the streets, which requires plenty of physical activity. And if you’re looking for a new space for a professional photoshoot in Vancouver, check it out.

Get lost in a dreamy loft garden oasis in Mount Pleasant

Nearly 200 lush plants will add botanical charm to any pic you shoot here. All you have to do is kick back beneath the twinkle lights and watch the likes pour in. It’s no surprise this is among our most beloved photoshoot ideas in Vancouver.

Admire Mother Nature at Queen Elizabeth Park

Want to showcase the beauty of nature on your IG page? Queen Elizabeth Park is Vancouver’s premier destination for outdoor lovers. Countless flowers, sprawling green fields, and landscaped gardens will breathe fresh air into your pics. The park also houses the Bloedel Conservatory, which is home to countless species of tropical plants and and exotic parrots! .

Take a stroll in Ambleside Park in West Vancouver

One of the most popular parks along Vancouver’s northside, Ambleside’s beachy aesthetic and gorgeous green fields make it ideal for those who want to shoot outdoors. You’re sure to find somewhere to snap an IG-worthy shot on one of Ambleside’s numerous nature walkways and seaside trails all year long.

Catch breathtaking views at the Vancouver Lookout

Can’t decide which part of the city you want to feature in your shot? At the Vancouver Lookout, you can have the whole city as your backdrop! Located on top of the Harbor Center more than 550 feet in the sky, the Vancouver lookout provides jaw-dropping 360 degree views of Gastown, Coal Harbor, North Vancouver and numerous other neighborhoods in downtown Vancouver. There’s also a glass elevator ride to the top that’s perfect for a dazzling story!

Gastown

Gastown, so named for the 19th century saloon-keeper nicknamed “Gassy Jack” for his gift of the gab, is one of the city’s most historic and charming neighbourhoods. It is full of Victorian architecture, like the 1908 flatiron building Hotel Europe, art galleries and studios, and trendy dining and shopping spots.

Vancouver For YOU

Vancouver is a major city in western Canada, located in the Lower Mainland region of British Columbia. As the most populous city in the province, the 2021 census recorded 662,248 people in the city, up from 631,486 in 2016. The Greater Vancouver area had a population of 2.6 million in 2021, making it the third-largest metropolitan area in Canada.
